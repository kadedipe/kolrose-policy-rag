# Kolrose Limited - Business Travel Policy

**Document ID:** KOL-ADMIN-001
**Version:** 2.0
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024
**Department:** Administration
**Approved By:** Chief Operating Officer

## 1. Travel Policy Overview

### 1.1 Purpose
This policy establishes guidelines and procedures for business travel undertaken 
by Kolrose Limited employees to ensure cost-effectiveness, employee safety, and 
compliance with company standards while supporting business objectives across 
Nigeria and internationally.

### 1.2 Scope
This policy applies to:
- All domestic travel within Nigeria
- International travel for business purposes
- Travel for client meetings, project sites, conferences, and training
- Travel costs reimbursed by Kolrose Limited or billable to clients

### 1.3 Duty of Care
Kolrose Limited prioritizes employee safety during business travel. The company 
maintains comprehensive travel insurance and provides security guidance for 
travel to higher-risk locations.

## 2. Travel Authorization

### 2.1 Pre-Travel Approval
All business travel requires pre-approval:

| Travel Type | Approval Required | Notice Period |
|-------------|------------------|---------------|
| Domestic (within FCT) | Line Manager | 2 working days |
| Domestic (other states) | Department Head | 5 working days |
| ECOWAS Countries | Director | 10 working days |
| International (non-ECOWAS) | Managing Director | 21 working days |

### 2.2 Travel Request Process
1. Employee submits Travel Request Form via Admin Portal
2. Form includes: destination, dates, purpose, estimated costs, client billing info
3. Manager reviews business necessity and budget availability
4. Admin department checks travel advisories for destination
5. Upon approval, Admin books travel through preferred vendors
6. Travel advance requested (if needed) - processed 3 days before departure

### 2.3 Group Travel
When multiple employees attend same event:
- One senior employee designated as travel coordinator
- Shared accommodation where practical (same gender)
- Group ground transportation arranged where possible
- Per diem rates unchanged

## 3. Transportation

### 3.1 Air Travel Standards

#### 3.1.1 Flight Booking
- Booked through preferred travel agency: Swift Travels Nigeria Ltd
- Economy class: Flights under 4 hours
- Premium Economy: Flights 4-8 hours (international)
- Business Class: Flights exceeding 8 hours OR Director-level and above
- Lowest logical fare principle applies (reasonable routing at best available rate)

#### 3.1.2 Preferred Airlines
Domestic:
- Air Peace
- Ibom Air
- Arik Air
- Dana Air (daytime flights only)

International:
- Emirates (Middle East, Asia connections)
- Ethiopian Airlines (Africa-wide coverage)
- British Airways (Europe, Americas)
- Turkish Airlines (Europe, Asia)
- Lufthansa (Europe)

#### 3.1.3 Booking Timeline
| Travel Type | Advance Booking Requirement |
|-------------|---------------------------|
| Domestic | Minimum 7 days |
| Regional (ECOWAS) | Minimum 14 days |
| International | Minimum 21 days |
| Emergency Travel | ASAP with Director approval |

Late bookings (within policy timelines) incurring premium fares require written 
justification.

### 3.2 Ground Transportation

#### 3.2.1 Airport Transfers
- Hotel shuttle: Preferred when available (free)
- Ride-hailing (Uber/Bolt): Approved (receipt required)
- Airport taxi: Maximum rates apply (Abuja: ₦5,000, Lagos: ₦8,000)
- Airport parking: Reimbursable for trips under 3 days (₦2,000/day maximum)

#### 3.2.2 Personal Vehicle for Business Travel
Employees using personal vehicles for business travel (beyond daily commute) receive:

| Component | Rate |
|-----------|------|
| Fuel & Maintenance | ₦350 per kilometer |
| Toll Charges | Actual cost with receipts |
| Parking | Actual cost with receipts |
| Driver Allowance | ₦5,000 per day (if employee provides driver) |

**Conditions:**
- Vehicle must be roadworthy and insured
- Employee must hold valid Nigerian driver's license
- Travel beyond 200km one-way requires pre-approval
- Inter-state travel: Public transportation or company vehicle preferred

#### 3.2.3 Vehicle Hire/Rental
- Pre-arranged through Admin Department
- Preferred vendor: Hertz Nigeria or Avis Nigeria
- Compact/economy class for solo travel
- SUV for group travel or rough terrain destinations
- Fuel costs reimbursed with receipts
- Insurance and CDW (Collision Damage Waiver) mandatory

## 4. Accommodation

### 4.1 Hotel Standards
Hotel category by employee level and location:

| Employee Level | FCT/Lagos | Other State Capitals | Other Towns |
|----------------|-----------|---------------------|-------------|
| Director & Above | 4-Star (₦50,000 max) | 4-Star (₦40,000 max) | Best Available (₦30,000) |
| Manager/Senior Staff | 3-Star (₦35,000 max) | 3-Star (₦25,000 max) | Best Available (₦20,000) |
| Other Employees | 3-Star (₦25,000 max) | Budget Hotel (₦15,000) | Budget Hotel (₦12,000) |

### 4.2 Accommodation Booking
- Booked through Admin Department or approved travel agency
- Room only rate (breakfast may be included if complimentary)
- Incidentals (mini-bar, pay-TV, laundry) are personal expenses
- Hotel loyalty points earned belong to employee (not company)
- Check-out time: Standard 12:00 PM; late check-out if flight schedule requires

### 4.3 Long-Stay Accommodation
For travel exceeding 14 consecutive nights:
- Serviced apartment or Airbnb preferred (cost comparison required)
- Kitchen facilities enable self-catering (reduced meal allowance)
- Weekly cleaning service allowance included
- Maximum monthly accommodation budget: 150% of equivalent hotel rate × 30 days

### 4.4 Accommodation with Family/Friends
- No accommodation reimbursement if staying with family or friends
- Token gift for host: Up to ₦15,000 reimbursable with receipt
- Must still meet all business obligations regardless of accommodation type

## 5. Per Diem and Meals

### 5.1 Daily Meal Allowance
Meal allowances when travel requires overnight stay:

| Location Tier | Breakfast | Lunch | Dinner | Full Day Total |
|---------------|-----------|-------|--------|-----------------|
| Abuja/Lagos | ₦3,000 | ₦5,000 | ₦8,000 | ₦16,000 |
| Other State Capitals | ₦2,500 | ₦4,000 | ₦6,500 | ₦13,000 |
| Other Locations | ₦2,000 | ₦3,000 | ₦5,000 | ₦10,000 |
| International (High-Cost)* | ₦5,000 | ₦8,000 | ₦12,000 | ₦25,000 |
| International (Standard) | ₦3,500 | ₦6,000 | ₦9,000 | ₦18,500 |

*High-cost cities: London, New York, Dubai, Geneva, Singapore

### 5.2 Meal Allowance Conditions
- Per diem is a maximum, not an entitlement (spend what is needed)
- Receipts required for meals exceeding ₦5,000
- Meals provided at conference/event reduce per diem accordingly
- Travel days: 75% of full day rate for partial travel days
- Alcohol: Maximum 2 drinks with dinner; not reimbursable at lunch

### 5.3 Client Meals
When dining with clients:
- More flexible budget allowed (reasonableness test)
- Receipt with attendees list and business purpose required
- Client meals claimed separately from per diem (not both)
- Senior person at meal pays (or host organization rotation)

## 6. Travel Advances and Reconciliation

### 6.1 Travel Cash Advance
Employees may request travel advance (estimated out-of-pocket expenses):

| Travel Duration | Maximum Advance |
|-----------------|-----------------|
| 1-3 days | ₦50,000 |
| 4-7 days | ₦100,000 |
| 8-14 days | ₦200,000 |
| 15+ days | ₦300,000 |

- Advances requested minimum 3 working days before departure
- Paid via bank transfer to employee's salary account
- Outstanding advances from previous trips must be reconciled before new advance

### 6.2 Expense Reconciliation
Post-travel expense reporting:

| Travel Type | Reconciliation Deadline |
|-------------|------------------------|
| Domestic | 7 calendar days after return |
| International | 14 calendar days after return |

Late reconciliation results in:
- Warning for first offense
- Loss of travel advance privilege for second offense
- Deduction from salary for third offense (unreconciled advance treated as debt)

### 6.3 Expense Report Requirements
Complete expense report includes:
- Travel Expense Report Form (signed by employee and manager)
- All receipts organized by date and category
- Trip report summarizing business outcomes (for trips exceeding 3 days)
- Return of unspent advance funds (within reconciliation deadline)
- Client billing breakdown (if client-reimbursable expenses included)

## 7. International Travel Specifics

### 7.1 Passport and Visa
- Employees must possess valid international passport (minimum 6 months validity)
- Visa application costs reimbursed upon successful issuance
- Admin department assists with visa application documentation
- Visa processing time factored into travel planning
- Denied visa application costs reimbursed (one attempt per trip)

### 7.2 Travel Insurance
Company-provided travel insurance covers:
- Medical emergencies and evacuation
- Trip cancellation/interruption
- Lost luggage and personal effects
- Personal accident
- 24/7 emergency assistance hotline

Insurance certificate provided with travel documents.

### 7.3 Health Preparations
- Required vaccinations at company expense
- Yellow fever certificate mandatory (Nigerian requirement)
- COVID-19 requirements per destination country
- Anti-malarial medication for travel to malaria-endemic areas
- Pre-existing medical conditions should be disclosed to Admin (confidentially)

### 7.4 Security Considerations
- Register with Nigerian Embassy/Consulate at destination (for trips over 7 days)
- Company security briefing for travel to higher-risk countries
- Daily check-in protocol with designated office contact
- Emergency evacuation plan reviewed before travel
- Travel to high-risk countries requires Managing Director approval

## 8. Travel During Security Advisories

### 8.1 Domestic Travel Advisories
When Nigerian government issues travel advisories for specific states/regions:
- Travel to affected areas suspended until advisory lifted
- Alternative arrangements (virtual meetings, client travel to Abuja) explored
- In-progress travel reviewed for early return or relocation
- Employee safety overrides business considerations

### 8.2 Force Majeure Events
Company not liable for travel disruptions due to:
- Natural disasters
- Civil unrest or terrorism
- Pandemic/epidemic restrictions
- Airline bankruptcies or strikes
- Government travel bans

Reasonable additional costs reimbursed for safety-related changes to itinerary.
