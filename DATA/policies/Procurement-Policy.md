# Kolrose Limited - Procurement Policy

**Document ID:** KOL-FIN-002
**Version:** 2.0
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024
**Department:** Finance / Administration
**Approved By:** Chief Financial Officer

## 1. Policy Objectives and Principles

### 1.1 Objectives
This procurement policy ensures that Kolrose Limited:
- Obtains best value for money in all purchases
- Maintains transparency and fairness in procurement processes
- Complies with Nigerian procurement regulations (Public Procurement Act 2007 where applicable)
- Prevents fraud, corruption, and conflicts of interest
- Supports local and diverse suppliers where commercially viable

### 1.2 Core Principles
All procurement activities must adhere to:
- **Value for Money**: Optimal combination of quality, cost, and lifecycle value
- **Transparency**: Clear processes, documented decisions, auditable records
- **Fairness**: Equal opportunity for qualified suppliers
- **Integrity**: Zero tolerance for corruption or unethical practices
- **Competition**: Competitive procurement unless exception justified

### 1.3 Scope
This policy covers all purchases of goods, services, and works, including:
- IT equipment and software licenses
- Office supplies and equipment
- Professional services and consulting
- Facilities maintenance and improvements
- Project-specific procurement for client deliverables

## 2. Procurement Thresholds and Methods

### 2.1 Procurement Thresholds
Procurement method determined by estimated total value:

| Value Range (₦) | Procurement Method | Approvals Required |
|-----------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| 0 - 100,000 | Direct Purchase | Line Manager |
| 100,001 - 500,000 | 3 Verbal/Email Quotes | Department Head |
| 500,001 - 2,000,000 | 3 Written Quotes | Director |
| 2,000,001 - 10,000,000 | Formal Tender (3-5 bidders) | Director + CFO |
| Above 10,000,000 | Open Tender | Managing Director + Board |

### 2.2 Direct Purchase (Below ₦100,000)
- No competitive quotes required
- Market price reasonableness check
- Receipt and delivery confirmation required
- Monthly review of direct purchases by Department Head
- Splitting orders to stay under threshold is prohibited (treated as fraud)

### 2.3 Three-Quotes Method (₦100,001 - ₦2,000,000)
- Minimum 3 quotes from different suppliers
- Quotes documented in Quote Comparison Sheet
- Award to lowest compliant bidder (unless justified otherwise)
- "Compliant" means meeting specifications, delivery timeline, and terms

Exceptions to lowest bidder award require written justification:
- Superior quality or warranty provisions
- Past performance with supplier
- Urgent delivery requirements
- After-sales support and local presence

### 2.4 Formal Tender (₦2,000,001 - ₦10,000,000)
Process requirements:
1. Tender document prepared with clear specifications
2. Minimum 3-5 qualified bidders invited
3. Tender opening committee (minimum 3 persons, including Finance)
4. Technical evaluation against weighted criteria
5. Financial evaluation
6. Combined scoring matrix
7. Recommendation memo to approving authority
8. Contract award and notification to successful/unsuccessful bidders

### 2.5 Open Tender (Above ₦10,000,000)
As formal tender plus:
- Public advertisement (company website, at least 2 national newspapers)
- Minimum 21-day bid submission period
- Pre-qualification stage for complex procurement
- Bid security/bond may be required from bidders
- Board-level procurement committee oversight
- Post-award debriefing available for unsuccessful bidders

## 3. Supplier Management

### 3.1 Supplier Registration and Prequalification
Suppliers must provide:
- Certificate of Incorporation (CAC registration)
- Tax Identification Number (TIN)
- Tax Clearance Certificate (for contracts above ₦500,000)
- PENCOM compliance certificate (for companies with 3+ employees)
- ITF compliance certificate
- NSITF registration
- Evidence of similar past performance (3 references)
- Bank reference letter

### 3.2 Approved Supplier List
Admin Department maintains approved supplier list:
- Updated quarterly
- Based on past performance, pricing, and reliability
- Performance rating system (1-5 stars)
- Suppliers rated below 3 stars placed on probation
- Dormant suppliers (no transaction in 12 months) removed after review

### 3.3 Supplier Performance Evaluation
Evaluated semi-annually on:

| Criterion | Weight |
|-----------|--------|
| Quality of goods/services | 30% |
| Delivery timeliness | 25% |
| Price competitiveness | 20% |
| Customer service/responsiveness | 15% |
| Invoice accuracy | 10% |

### 3.4 Local Content Preference
In compliance with Nigerian Local Content Act:
- Preference for Nigerian-registered companies
- 10% price preference margin for local manufacturers
- Joint venture with Nigerian company required for foreign suppliers (where applicable)
- Technology transfer component encouraged for foreign procurement

## 4. Purchase Order Process

### 4.1 Requisition
1. Employee identifies need and completes Purchase Requisition Form
2. Line Manager approves business necessity
3. Admin/Finance confirms budget availability
4. Procurement method determined based on estimated value

### 4.2 Purchase Order Issuance
1. Approved requisition converted to Purchase Order (PO)
2. PO issued to selected supplier (with terms and conditions)
3. Supplier confirms acceptance of PO
4. Goods/services delivery scheduled
5. PO amendments require same approval as original

### 4.3 Receipt and Inspection
- Goods received by Admin/requisitioning department
- Delivery Note checked against PO and actual items
- Quality inspection completed (technical items: IT/Engineering review)
- Goods Received Note (GRN) issued
- Discrepancies documented and resolved with supplier within 48 hours

### 4.4 Invoice Processing and Payment
1. Supplier invoice matched with PO and GRN (three-way match)
2. Discrepancies resolved before payment
3. Approved invoices processed per payment terms
4. Standard payment terms: 30 days from invoice receipt

Payment terms exceptions:
- New/small suppliers: 14 days (relationship building)
- Government-mandated timelines: Compliance required
- Early payment discounts evaluated case-by-case

## 5. Contract Management

### 5.1 Contract Requirements
Written contracts required for:
- All procurement above ₦500,000
- Any procurement involving ongoing services
- Software licenses and subscriptions
- Consultancy engagements

### 5.2 Contract Content
Contracts must address:
- Clear scope of work/deliverables
- Timeline and milestones
- Payment schedule (linked to deliverables)
- Warranty and defect liability period
- Termination clauses
- Dispute resolution (mediation before litigation)
- Governing law (Federal Republic of Nigeria)
- Confidentiality and data protection
- Intellectual property rights (work-for-hire principle)

### 5.3 Contract Approval and Signing
Signing authority:

| Contract Value | Authorized Signatory |
|----------------|---------------------|
| Up to ₦2,000,000 | Department Head |
| ₦2,000,001 - ₦10,000,000 | Director or CFO |
| Above ₦10,000,000 | Managing Director (+ Board resolution if above ₦50M) |

Legal review required for contracts above ₦5,000,000 or involving intellectual property.

### 5.4 Contract Variations
- Variations exceeding 10% of original contract value require re-approval
- Cumulative variations exceeding 25% require new procurement process
- Variation orders must be in writing before work proceeds
- Verbal variations not binding on Kolrose Limited

## 6. Prohibited Practices

### 6.1 Fraud and Corruption
The following are strictly prohibited and constitute gross misconduct:
- Splitting orders to avoid competitive procurement thresholds
- Collusion with suppliers to manipulate pricing
- Accepting kickbacks, bribes, or secret commissions
- Falsifying quotations, delivery notes, or invoices
- Using company procurement for personal benefit
- Awarding contracts to relatives without disclosure and approval
- Sharing confidential bid information with favored suppliers

### 6.2 Conflict of Interest
- Employees must declare any personal interest in potential suppliers
- Family members/close associates of employees may bid but employee must recuse
- Post-employment restrictions: Former employees cannot bid for 12 months
- Gifts from suppliers: Governed by Code of Conduct Policy (KOL-HR-003)

### 6.3 Consequences of Violations
- Immediate suspension pending investigation
- Disciplinary action up to termination
- Criminal prosecution for fraud (EFCC referral)
- Recovery of losses from employee or supplier
- Supplier debarment (permanent blacklisting)
