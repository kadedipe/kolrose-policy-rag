# Kolrose Limited - Grievance and Dispute Resolution Policy

**Document ID:** KOL-HR-008
**Version:** 2.0
**Effective Date:** January 15, 2024
**Department:** Human Resources
**Approved By:** Managing Director & Legal Counsel

## 1. Policy Purpose and Principles

### 1.1 Policy Purpose
This policy provides a fair, transparent, and timely mechanism for employees to 
raise workplace concerns and have them addressed appropriately. Kolrose Limited 
is committed to maintaining a positive work environment where employees feel safe 
to voice concerns without fear of retaliation.

### 1.2 Guiding Principles
- **Fairness**: All parties treated impartially and given opportunity to be heard
- **Confidentiality**: Information shared only on need-to-know basis
- **Timeliness**: Matters addressed promptly at each stage
- **Non-Retaliation**: Zero tolerance for victimization of those raising grievances
- **Natural Justice**: Right to know the case against you and respond
- **Proportionality**: Outcomes proportionate to findings

### 1.3 Scope
This policy covers grievances related to:
- Terms and conditions of employment
- Workplace relationships (colleagues, managers, subordinates)
- Working environment and conditions
- Discrimination or harassment
- Unfair treatment or inconsistent application of policies
- Health and safety concerns
- Whistleblowing/reporting wrongdoing

**Excluded Matters:**
- Performance ratings (addressed via Performance Management Policy KOL-HR-006)
- Salary levels (addressed via annual salary review process)
- Redundancy/restructuring decisions (separate consultation process)
- Criminal matters (reported directly to law enforcement)

### 1.4 Grievance vs. Whistleblowing
| Aspect | Grievance | Whistleblowing |
|--------|-----------|----------------|
| Nature | Personal issue affecting employee | Reporting wrongdoing affecting company/public interest |
| Reporter | Affected employee | Any person with information |
| Process | This policy | Whistleblowing Policy (separate) |
| Confidentiality | Confidential to parties involved | Anonymous reporting available |

## 2. Informal Resolution

### 2.1 Encouragement of Informal Resolution
Employees are encouraged to resolve workplace concerns informally where possible 
before initiating formal grievance procedures. Many issues arise from 
misunderstandings that can be resolved through direct conversation.

### 2.2 Informal Resolution Steps
1. Employee reflects on issue: What specifically is the concern? What resolution is sought?
2. Employee requests private meeting with the other party
3. Discussion focuses on issue (not personality), uses "I feel" statements
4. Mutually acceptable resolution agreed
5. Email summary of understanding (optional but recommended)
6. If unresolved or inappropriate for direct discussion, move to formal process

### 2.3 When Informal Resolution is Inappropriate
Informal resolution should NOT be attempted when:
- The grievance involves harassment, discrimination, or bullying
- There is a significant power imbalance
- The employee feels unsafe or intimidated
- The matter is already formal or involves serious misconduct
- Previous informal attempts have failed

### 2.4 Management Intervention (Optional)
Before formal grievance, employee may request that their line manager or HR 
mediate an informal discussion. Manager acts as neutral facilitator, not judge.

## 3. Formal Grievance Procedure

### 3.1 Stage 1: Written Grievance
Employee submits written grievance to HR (grievances@kolroselimited.com.ng) containing:
- Employee name, department, and position
- Date of incident(s) or when concern arose
- Detailed description of the issue (facts, not opinions)
- Names of any witnesses
- Evidence or documentation supporting the grievance
- Attempts at informal resolution and their outcomes
- Desired resolution or outcome
- Signature and date

### 3.2 Stage 2: Acknowledgment and Initial Review
Upon receipt of written grievance:
1. HR acknowledges receipt within 2 working days
2. HR reviews grievance for completeness and policy coverage
3. HR assigns Grievance Officer (neutral, not involved in the matter)
4. Grievance Officer determines if matter falls within policy scope
5. If grievance is about HR staff, matter escalated to Director level
6. Employee notified of assigned Grievance Officer within 3 working days

### 3.3 Stage 3: Investigation
Grievance Officer conducts investigation:
1. Reviews written grievance and supporting documents
2. Interviews the grievant (employee raising grievance)
   - Employee may be accompanied by colleague or union representative
   - Interview conducted within 5 working days of assignment
3. Notifies respondent (person complained about) of allegations
   - Written summary provided (may be redacted to protect confidentiality)
   - Respondent given opportunity to respond in writing
4. Interviews respondent (within 5 working days of notification)
   - Respondent may be accompanied by colleague
5. Interviews witnesses identified by both parties
6. Collects and reviews additional evidence (emails, documents, CCTV if relevant)
7. Investigation completed within 10-15 working days (may be extended with notice)

### 3.4 Stage 4: Findings and Decision
Grievance Officer prepares report including:
- Summary of allegations and responses
- Evidence reviewed
- Findings of fact (on balance of probabilities)
- Policy/procedure analysis
- Conclusion on whether grievance is:
  - **Upheld**: Grievance found valid
  - **Partially Upheld**: Some elements valid, others not
  - **Not Upheld**: Grievance not substantiated
  - **Inconclusive**: Insufficient evidence either way
- Recommended remedies or actions

Report submitted to Head of HR and relevant Director for endorsement within 5 
working days of completing investigation.

### 3.5 Stage 5: Communication of Outcome
Employee informed of outcome in writing within 3 working days of decision:
- Summary of findings
- Decision on each element of grievance
- Remedies or actions to be taken (with timeline)
- Right of appeal and process
- Confirmation that no retaliation will be tolerated

Meeting offered to discuss outcome in person (employee may bring companion).

## 4. Remedies and Outcomes

### 4.1 Possible Remedies (If Grievance Upheld)
Depending on nature of grievance, remedies may include:

| Category | Possible Remedies |
|----------|-------------------|
| Interpersonal | Mediation, apology, separation of parties, training |
| Management Practice | Coaching for manager, policy clarification, practice change |
| Working Conditions | Equipment provision, workplace adjustment, schedule change |
| Discrimination/Harassment | Disciplinary action against perpetrator, policy training, compensation |
| Procedural Error | Decision reconsideration, backdating of benefits, correction of records |
| Financial | Back pay, reimbursement, compensation (per legal advice) |

### 4.2 Implementation
- Responsible person identified for each remedy
- Timeline set (typically 5-30 days depending on complexity)
- HR tracks implementation and confirms completion
- Employee notified when all remedies implemented

### 4.3 If Grievance Not Upheld
- Employee provided with clear reasons for decision
- Counseling or support offered if employee distressed by outcome
- No record on employee file (grievance filed separately from personnel record)
- Employee retains all normal employment rights

## 5. Appeal Process

### 5.1 Grounds for Appeal
Employee may appeal grievance outcome on following grounds:
- Procedural error in grievance handling
- New evidence not available during investigation
- Decision unreasonable based on evidence
- Remedy inadequate given findings
- Bias or conflict of interest in Grievance Officer

### 5.2 Appeal Submission
- Written appeal submitted to Head of HR within 7 working days of receiving outcome
- Appeal must specify ground(s) and provide supporting information
- Appeal heard by person senior to original decision-maker (typically Director level)
- Appeal panel constituted if matter is complex: Director + HR + Independent person

### 5.3 Appeal Process
1. Appeal acknowledged within 2 working days
2. Appeal hearing scheduled within 10 working days
3. Employee presents case (may bring companion)
4. Original decision-maker may be called to explain decision
5. Appeal decision is final and binding internally
6. Employee notified in writing within 5 working days of hearing

### 5.4 External Options
After exhausting internal process, employee retains right to:
- Seek legal advice
- File complaint with Federal Ministry of Labour and Employment
- Pursue civil remedy through courts (breach of contract, etc.)
- Note: Mediation clause in employment contract may apply

## 6. Special Grievance Categories

### 6.1 Harassment and Discrimination Grievances
Special provisions apply:
- Investigation prioritized and fast-tracked
- Employee may bypass line manager and report directly to HR or Director
- Options for interim measures (temporary separation, work-from-home)
- External investigator may be engaged for serious cases
- Victim support services provided (counseling, legal referral)
- Confidentiality strictly maintained; breaches treated as misconduct

### 6.2 Collective Grievances
When multiple employees share same grievance:
- Employees nominate 2-3 representatives
- Grievance submitted as joint document with all names
- Single investigation covering all grievants
- Communication via representatives unless individual meetings required
- Individual outcomes may vary based on personal circumstances

### 6.3 Grievances During Probation
Probationary employees have full access to grievance procedure. However:
- Does not extend probation period (unless grievance about probation assessment)
- Does not create permanent employment rights beyond probation terms
- Retaliation protection applies equally to probationary employees

### 6.4 Grievances After Employment
Former employees may raise grievances within 30 days of termination regarding:
- Final pay or benefits
- Alleged discrimination contributing to termination
- References or certificates not provided

## 7. Records and Confidentiality

### 7.1 Record-Keeping
Grievance records maintained securely by HR:
- Formal grievance file created (separate from personnel file)
- All documents, notes, evidence, and decisions included
- Appeal documents filed with original grievance
- Retention: 3 years after case closure, then securely destroyed
- Exceptions: Legal hold, ongoing proceedings, pattern evidence

### 7.2 Confidentiality Obligations
- All parties instructed on confidentiality at outset
- Information shared only on strict need-to-know basis
- Breach of confidentiality by any party treated as misconduct
- Witnesses specifically instructed not to discuss case
- Anonymous investigation notes permitted to protect witnesses

### 7.3 Data Protection
Grievance records contain personal and sensitive data:
- Handled per NDPR (Nigeria Data Protection Regulation)
- Employees have right to access their own grievance records
- Third-party personal data may be redacted from access copies
- Data processed only for legitimate grievance handling purpose

## 8. Anti-Retaliation Protection

### 8.1 Zero Tolerance
Kolrose Limited prohibits retaliation against any employee who:
- Raises a grievance in good faith
- Participates in a grievance investigation as witness
- Supports a colleague in grievance process
- Appeals a grievance outcome

### 8.2 Forms of Retaliation
Retaliation includes but is not limited to:
- Dismissal, demotion, or suspension
- Negative performance evaluation without basis
- Exclusion from opportunities (training, promotion, projects)
- Hostile behavior, ostracism, or intimidation
- Unfavorable changes to duties or working conditions

### 8.3 Reporting Retaliation
- Reported immediately to Head of HR or any Director
- Investigated as separate grievance with priority
- Confirmed retaliation is gross misconduct (summary dismissal)
- Interim protection measures implemented during investigation

### 8.4 False or Vexatious Grievances
Protection does not extend to grievances found to be:
- Knowingly false or malicious
- Brought solely to harass or harm another employee
- Repeated without new evidence after previous dismissal

Making knowingly false allegations is itself misconduct subject to disciplinary action.
