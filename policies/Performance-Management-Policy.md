# Kolrose Limited - Performance Management Policy

**Document ID:** KOL-HR-006
**Version:** 3.0
**Effective Date:** January 15, 2024
**Department:** Human Resources

## 1. Performance Management Framework

### 1.1 Philosophy
Kolrose Limited believes in continuous performance development through regular 
feedback, clear goal-setting, and fair evaluation. Our performance management 
system aims to:
- Align individual performance with organizational objectives
- Identify and develop talent for career growth
- Provide fair basis for compensation and promotion decisions
- Address performance gaps constructively

### 1.2 Performance Cycle
The performance year runs from January 1 to December 31, with the following cycle:

| Period | Activity |
|--------|----------|
| January | Goal Setting for current year |
| April | Q1 Informal Check-in |
| July | Mid-Year Formal Review |
| October | Q3 Informal Check-in |
| December | Year-End Performance Appraisal |
| January | Performance Discussion and New Goals |

## 2. Goal Setting

### 2.1 SMART Goals Framework
All performance goals must follow the SMART framework:
- **Specific**: Clear, well-defined objective
- **Measurable**: Quantifiable metrics for success
- **Achievable**: Realistic given resources and constraints
- **Relevant**: Aligned with department and company objectives
- **Time-bound**: Clear deadline for completion

### 2.2 Goal Categories
Employees set 5-7 goals annually across these categories:

| Category | Weight | Description |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| Business Objectives | 50% | Core job responsibilities and deliverables |
| Project Goals | 20% | Specific project milestones and outcomes |
| Professional Development | 15% | Skills acquisition, certifications, learning |
| Values & Behaviors | 15% | Demonstration of company values |

### 2.3 Goal Approval and Modification
- Goals drafted by employee, reviewed with line manager
- Finalized by January 31
- Modifications require mutual agreement and HR notification
- Changes after September 30 only in exceptional circumstances

## 3. Performance Ratings

### 3.1 Rating Scale

| Rating | Score Range | Description |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| Outstanding (O) | 4.5 - 5.0 | Consistently exceeds expectations; exceptional achievements |
| Exceeds Expectations (EE) | 3.5 - 4.4 | Frequently exceeds expectations; strong performer |
| Meets Expectations (ME) | 2.5 - 3.4 | Consistently meets requirements; solid performer |
| Needs Improvement (NI) | 1.5 - 2.4 | Does not consistently meet expectations |
| Unsatisfactory (U) | 1.0 - 1.4 | Significantly below expectations |

### 3.2 Rating Distribution Guidelines
Expected rating distribution for teams of 10+ employees:
- Outstanding: 5-10% of team
- Exceeds Expectations: 15-20% of team
- Meets Expectations: 60-70% of team
- Needs Improvement/Unsatisfactory: 5-10% of team

Deviations from distribution require Director justification.

### 3.3 Calibration Process
- Department heads calibrate ratings across teams (January 15-20)
- Ensures consistency in rating standards
- Prevents rating inflation or bias
- Final ratings approved by Director before communication to employees

## 4. Performance Review Process

### 4.1 Self-Assessment
Employees complete self-assessment addressing:
- Achievement against each goal (with evidence)
- Key accomplishments beyond goals
- Challenges faced and solutions implemented
- Skills developed during the year
- Areas for improvement
- Goals and aspirations for next year

Self-assessment submitted 1 week before review meeting.

### 4.2 Manager Assessment
Line managers prepare assessment including:
- Evaluation against each goal with evidence
- 360-degree feedback summary (collected from peers, direct reports, clients)
- Competency assessment against role requirements
- Overall rating with justification
- Development recommendations

### 4.3 Performance Discussion Meeting
The review meeting between employee and manager covers:
1. Review of achievements and challenges
2. Discussion of ratings with specific examples
3. Strengths and areas for improvement
4. Career aspirations and development needs
5. Goals and development plan for next year
6. Employee comments and signature

Meeting duration: Minimum 1 hour, uninterrupted.

### 4.4 Performance Documentation
Signed performance review documents stored in employee personnel file. Employee 
receives copy within 5 working days of discussion.

## 5. Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

### 5.1 PIP Initiation Criteria
A Performance Improvement Plan may be initiated when:
- Employee receives "Needs Improvement" or "Unsatisfactory" rating
- Manager identifies specific, persistent performance gaps
- New role requirements reveal skill gaps (with support commitment)

### 5.2 PIP Structure
PIP duration: 60-90 calendar days, including:

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Specific Objectives | 3-5 measurable improvement goals |
| Support Plan | Training, mentoring, resources provided |
| Check-in Schedule | Weekly progress reviews with manager |
| Success Criteria | Clear metrics for PIP completion |
| Consequences | Outcome if PIP unsuccessful |

### 5.3 PIP Outcomes

| Outcome | Action |
|---------|--------|
| Successful | PIP closed; employee returns to normal performance cycle |
| Partial Success | PIP extended by 30 days (one extension only) |
| Unsuccessful | Termination of employment or demotion (at company discretion) |

### 5.4 Employee Rights During PIP
- Clear written explanation of performance concerns
- Opportunity to respond and provide context
- Access to promised support and resources
- Right to be accompanied in formal PIP meetings
- Appeal process if PIP is believed to be unfair

## 6. Performance-Related Decisions

### 6.1 Annual Salary Review
Performance ratings impact annual salary adjustments:

| Rating | Salary Increase Range |
|--------|----------------------|
| Outstanding | 15% - 25% |
| Exceeds Expectations | 8% - 14% |
| Meets Expectations | 3% - 7% |
| Needs Improvement | 0% - 2% |
| Unsatisfactory | 0% |

### 6.2 Bonus Eligibility
Annual performance bonus (if declared by company):

| Rating | Bonus Percentage of Annual Salary |
|--------|-----------------------------------|
| Outstanding | 30% - 50% |
| Exceeds Expectations | 15% - 25% |
| Meets Expectations | 5% - 10% |
| Below ME | Not eligible |

### 6.3 Promotion Consideration
- Minimum "Exceeds Expectations" rating for promotion consideration
- At least 2 years in current role (unless exceptional)
- Successful completion of any required certifications
- Interview with next-level manager and HR
- Promotion effective January 1 or July 1 only
