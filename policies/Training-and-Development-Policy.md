# Kolrose Limited - Training and Professional Development Policy

**Document ID:** KOL-HR-007
**Version:** 2.1
**Effective Date:** January 15, 2024
**Department:** Human Resources
**Approved By:** Managing Director

## 1. Policy Framework

### 1.1 Philosophy
Kolrose Limited is committed to developing our employees' skills and capabilities 
as a strategic investment in our company's future. We believe that continuous 
learning is essential for maintaining our competitive edge in Nigeria's technology 
sector and for providing meaningful career paths for our employees.

### 1.2 Objectives
This policy aims to:
- Build technical and professional capabilities aligned with business needs
- Support employee career growth and retention
- Ensure compliance with industry certifications and standards
- Foster a culture of continuous learning and knowledge sharing
- Maximize return on training investment through practical application

### 1.3 Scope
This policy applies to all confirmed (post-probation) full-time employees. 
Probationary employees are eligible for mandatory compliance training only.

## 2. Training Categories

### 2.1 Mandatory Compliance Training
All employees must complete the following training:

| Training Course | Frequency | Duration |
|-----------------|-----------|----------|
| Code of Conduct & Ethics | Annually | 2 hours |
| Information Security Awareness | Annually | 2 hours |
| Data Protection (NDPR) | Annually | 3 hours |
| Health & Safety at Work | Annually | 1 hour |
| Anti-Corruption & Bribery | Annually | 2 hours |

Completion tracked via HR Portal. Non-completion by deadline results in temporary 
system access suspension until training is completed.

### 2.2 Technical Skills Training
Role-specific technical training to maintain and enhance job capabilities:

**Technology Infrastructure Division:**
- Network certification (Cisco CCNA/CCNP)
- Cloud platform certifications (AWS, Azure, Google Cloud)
- Cybersecurity certifications (CompTIA Security+, CISSP, CEH)
- Annual training budget: ₦300,000 per technical employee

**Software Development Division:**
- Programming language advanced courses
- Agile/Scrum methodology certifications
- DevOps and CI/CD pipeline training
- Software architecture and design patterns
- Annual training budget: ₦300,000 per developer

**Project Management Office:**
- PMP (Project Management Professional) certification
- PRINCE2 certification
- Agile Project Management
- Government procurement procedures
- Annual training budget: ₦350,000 per project manager

### 2.3 Soft Skills Development
Cross-functional professional skills:

| Skill Area | Examples | Eligibility |
|------------|----------|-------------|
| Leadership & Management | Supervisory skills, team leadership | Team leads and above |
| Communication | Business writing, presentation skills | All employees |
| Client Management | Relationship building, negotiation | Client-facing roles |
| Time Management | Productivity, prioritization | All employees |
| Emotional Intelligence | Self-awareness, empathy, conflict resolution | All employees |

Budget: ₦100,000 per employee annually for soft skills training.

### 2.4 Professional Certification Support
Kolrose Limited supports professional certifications through:

| Support Type | Details |
|--------------|---------|
| Exam Fees | 100% coverage for pre-approved certifications (first attempt) |
| Study Materials | Up to ₦50,000 reimbursement |
| Study Leave | 5 working days per certification attempt |
| Renewal Fees | 50% coverage for ongoing certification maintenance |
| Bonus Award | ₦100,000 one-time bonus for achieving key certification |

**Conditions for Certification Support:**
- Certification must be relevant to current or next-level role
- Pre-approval required using Training Request Form
- Employee must pass within 2 attempts (3rd attempt at employee cost)
- If employee leaves within 12 months of certification, pro-rated repayment applies
- Certification must be maintained for duration of employment

|--------------|----------------------|--------------|

## 3. Training Request and Approval Process

### 3.1 Individual Training Requests
Process for employee-initiated training:

1. **Employee**: Identifies training need and discusses with line manager
2. **Quarterly Review**: Training needs discussed during quarterly check-in
3. **Training Request Form**: Submitted via HR Portal with business justification
4. **Manager Approval**: Within 5 working days
5. **Department Head Approval**: Required for training exceeding ₦100,000
6. **HR Review**: Ensures alignment with development plan and budget
7. **Booking**: Admin department handles logistics for approved training

### 3.2 Department Training Plans
Each department submits an annual training plan by January 31:
- Identifies critical skill gaps
- Prioritizes training investments
- Aligns with business objectives and project pipeline
- Includes estimated budget and timeline
- Consolidated by HR into company-wide training calendar

### 3.3 Training Budget Management
- Annual training budget allocated per department (typically 3-5% of payroll)
- Quarterly review of training expenditure vs. budget
- Unused Q1-Q2 budget may be carried forward to Q3
- Unused annual budget not carried over to next year (use it or lose it)
- Overspend requires Director approval with business justification

## 4. Post-Training Obligations

### 4.1 Knowledge Sharing
Within 14 calendar days of completing training, employee must:
- Deliver a 30-minute presentation to team/department (in-person or virtual)
- Share training materials via company knowledge base (SharePoint)
- Submit Training Effectiveness Evaluation Form to HR
- Identify 2-3 actionable improvements to implement in their work

### 4.2 Training Effectiveness Evaluation
Training impact assessed at three intervals:

| Timing | Evaluation Method |
|--------|-------------------|
| Immediately Post-Training | Participant feedback form (reaction) |
| 90 Days Post-Training | Manager assessment of skill application |
| Annual Review | Integration into performance evaluation |

### 4.3 Service Bonds and Repayment
Training investments requiring repayment if employee voluntarily resigns:

| Training Cost | Service Period | Repayment Formula |
|---------------|----------------|-------------------|
| Above ₦200,000 | 12 months | Pro-rated for months remaining |
| Above ₦500,000 | 24 months | Pro-rated for months remaining |
| Above ₦1,000,000 | 36 months | Pro-rated for months remaining |

Repayment deducted from final salary and benefits. Legal action pursued if 
outstanding balance exceeds ₦200,000.

## 5. Internal Training Programs

### 5.1 Lunch and Learn Sessions
- Weekly voluntary sessions (Wednesdays 1:00 PM - 2:00 PM)
- Topics range from technical skills to industry trends
- Presented by internal experts or external guests
- Lunch provided by company
- Attendance tracked for professional development records

### 5.2 Mentorship Program
- Formal mentorship program matching junior and senior employees
- 6-month mentoring cycles with monthly meetings (minimum)
- Mentors receive ₦20,000 monthly allowance
- Application process opens January and July
- Cross-departmental mentoring encouraged

### 5.3 Graduate Trainee Program
- 12-month structured program for fresh graduates
- Rotations across 3-4 departments (3 months each)
- Technical and soft skills training integrated
- Monthly stipend: ₦80,000 during training
- Successful completion leads to permanent employment
- Next intake: September 2024 (applications open May 2024)

### 5.4 Internship Program (IT/SIWES)
- Industrial Training placements in partnership with Nigerian universities
- Duration: 3-6 months
- Monthly stipend: ₦30,000
- Structured learning objectives and supervisor assignment
- Certificate of completion and reference letter provided
