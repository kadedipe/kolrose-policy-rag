# Kolrose Limited - Occupational Health and Safety Policy

**Document ID:** KOL-ADMIN-002
**Version:** 1.2
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024
**Department:** Administration
**Approved By:** Managing Director

## 1. Policy Statement

### 1.1 Commitment
Kolrose Limited is committed to providing a safe and healthy work environment for 
all employees, contractors, clients, and visitors. We believe that all workplace 
injuries and illnesses are preventable through proper planning, training, and 
vigilance.

### 1.2 Legal Compliance
This policy complies with:
- Nigerian Factories Act Cap F1 LFN 2004
- Employee's Compensation Act 2010
- National Environmental Standards and Regulations Enforcement Agency (NESREA) regulations
- Federal Fire Service regulations
- FCT Environmental Protection Board requirements

### 1.3 Responsibility Statement
While the company provides the framework and resources for safety, EVERY employee 
has personal responsibility for their own safety and that of colleagues. Safety is 
a condition of employment, not an optional extra.

## 2. Safety Organization

### 2.1 Health and Safety Committee
The committee meets monthly and comprises:

| Role | Position |
|------|----------|
| Chairperson | Head of Administration |
| Secretary | HR Officer (Safety) |
| Management Reps | One from each department |
| Employee Reps | Two elected by staff |
| Technical Advisor | Facilities Manager |

### 2.2 Safety Officers
- **Chief Safety Officer**: Head of Administration
- **Floor Wardens**: 2 employees per floor
- **First Aiders**: Minimum 2 trained first aiders always present during working hours
- **Fire Marshals**: 2 employees per floor

Training and refresher courses provided annually for all safety role holders.

### 2.3 Employee Safety Responsibilities
Every employee must:
- Follow all safety procedures and instructions
- Report hazards, near-misses, and incidents immediately
- Attend mandatory safety training
- Use personal protective equipment (PPE) where required
- Not interfere with or misuse safety equipment
- Not operate equipment they are not trained/authorized to use
- Cooperate with safety investigations and inspections

## 3. Workplace Safety Standards

### 3.1 General Office Safety
Suite 10, Bataiya Plaza must be maintained to the following standards:

| Area | Standard |
|------|----------|
| Walkways/Corridors | Clear, unobstructed, minimum 1 meter width |
| Emergency Exits | Clearly marked, illuminated, never blocked |
| Electrical Safety | No daisy-chained extensions; PAT tested annually |
| Lighting | Minimum 300 lux at workstations |
| Ventilation | Adequate airflow; AC maintained quarterly |
| Noise Levels | Below 85 dB; quiet zones for focused work |
| Housekeeping | Clean desk policy; waste bins emptied daily |

### 3.2 Workstation Ergonomics
- Ergonomic assessment within first week of employment
- Adjustable chairs with lumbar support provided
- Monitor at eye level, approximately arm's length distance
- Keyboard and mouse positioned for neutral wrist posture
- Footrests available on request
- Sit-stand desks available on medical recommendation
- Regular breaks encouraged: 5 minutes every hour

### 3.3 Display Screen Equipment (DSE) Safety
- Eye test provided annually for regular computer users
- Anti-glare screen filters provided if needed
- Regular reminders for the 20-20-20 rule (every 20 min, look 20 feet away, 20 seconds)
- Proper lighting to reduce glare and eye strain

## 4. Emergency Preparedness

### 4.1 Fire Safety
Fire safety measures include:

| Measure | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| Fire Extinguishers | Dry powder and CO2; inspected monthly |
| Smoke Detectors | All rooms and corridors; tested quarterly |
| Fire Alarm | Manual call points at exits; tested monthly |
| Emergency Lighting | Battery backup; tested quarterly |
| Evacuation Maps | Posted on every floor |
| Fire Drills | Conducted semi-annually (March & September) |
| Assembly Point | Bataiya Plaza car park, eastern corner |

### 4.2 Fire Prevention Rules
- Smoking only in designated outdoor area
- Electrical appliances switched off when not in use
- No space heaters or personal cooking appliances
- Flammable materials stored in fireproof cabinets
- Server room: Specialized fire suppression system (FM-200)

### 4.3 Emergency Evacuation Procedure
In event of fire or evacuation alarm:
1. Remain calm; leave belongings behind
2. Proceed to nearest exit (do not use elevators)
3. Floor wardens check all rooms and direct staff
4. Assist visitors and persons with disabilities
5. Proceed to assembly point
6. Department heads conduct head count
7. Report missing persons to Chief Safety Officer immediately
8. Do not re-enter building until "all clear" given by Fire Service

### 4.4 Medical Emergencies
- First aid boxes located at reception and kitchen areas
- Automated External Defibrillator (AED) at reception
- Emergency contact numbers posted by all phones
- Nearest hospital: Garki Hospital (5 minutes drive)
- Ambulance service: 112 (FCT Emergency) or 199 (FRSC)
- Incident report completed for all medical events

### 4.5 Security Incidents
In event of security threat (robbery, civil unrest):
- Follow instructions from security personnel
- Move to designated safe area if directed
- Lock office doors if safe to do so
- Contact company security: 0800-KOL-SEC (0800-565-732)
- Police emergency: 112 or 199
- Report all incidents to Admin Department within 1 hour

## 5. Health and Wellness

### 5.1 Occupational Health
- Pre-employment medical examination required
- Annual health screening for all employees (company-funded)
- Vaccination programs offered (flu, hepatitis, etc.)
- Health insurance covers outpatient and inpatient care (per Employee Benefits)
- Mental health support: 6 counseling sessions annually via EAP

### 5.2 Stress Management
- Workload monitoring in quarterly reviews
- Open-door policy for discussing stress-related concerns
- Flexible working arrangements available (per Remote Work Policy)
- Stress management workshops offered bi-annually
- Zero tolerance for workplace bullying or harassment

### 5.3 Workplace Hygiene
- Hand sanitizing stations at entrance and common areas
- Regular cleaning of shared surfaces (door handles, printers, etc.)
- Kitchen/canteen hygiene standards enforced
- Sick employees encouraged to stay home or work remotely
- Pest control treatment quarterly

### 5.4 Infectious Disease Protocol
In event of disease outbreak (as declared by NCDC):
- Follow Nigeria Centre for Disease Control guidelines
- Temperature checks at office entrance
- Enhanced cleaning and sanitization
- Remote work activated as primary work mode (if directed)
- Travel restrictions per government advisories
- Vaccination requirements per public health directives

## 6. Incident Reporting and Investigation

### 6.1 Incident Categories
Reportable incidents include:
- **Injury**: Any physical harm requiring first aid or medical treatment
- **Near Miss**: Event that could have caused injury but did not
- **Property Damage**: Damage to company or client property
- **Environmental**: Spills, emissions, or improper waste disposal
- **Security**: Theft, unauthorized access, threats
- **Health**: Occupational illness or exposure

### 6.2 Reporting Timeline

| Incident Severity | Reporting Deadline |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| Fatal or Major Injury | Within 1 hour to Admin + Authorities |
| Lost Time Injury | Within 4 hours to Admin |
| Minor Injury (First Aid) | Within 24 hours (Incident Report Form) |
| Near Miss | Within 24 hours (Incident Report Form) |
| Property Damage | Within 4 hours to Admin |

### 6.3 Investigation Process
For all injuries and significant near misses:
1. Incident scene secured and evidence preserved
2. Investigation team formed (Admin + Department Rep + Safety Officer)
3. Witness statements collected within 24 hours
4. Root cause analysis completed (5 Whys or Fishbone method)
5. Corrective actions identified with implementation timeline
6. Investigation report submitted within 5 working days
7. Actions tracked to completion; effectiveness verified

### 6.4 Workers' Compensation
In accordance with Employee's Compensation Act 2010:
- All injuries reported to Nigeria Social Insurance Trust Fund (NSITF)
- Medical expenses covered for work-related injuries
- Disability compensation per ECA schedule
- Death benefits for fatal workplace accidents
- Claims processed through HR within 14 days of incident

## 7. Contractor and Visitor Safety

### 7.1 Contractor Safety
Contractors working on company premises must:
- Provide evidence of own safety policy and insurance
- Sign in at reception and wear visitor badge
- Attend site safety induction (for work exceeding 1 day)
- Comply with all Kolrose Limited safety rules
- Report any incidents immediately to Admin

### 7.2 Visitor Safety
All visitors:
- Must be signed in by host employee
- Wear visitor badge at all times
- Be accompanied in restricted areas
- Briefed on emergency procedures by host
- Accounted for in evacuations by host employee
