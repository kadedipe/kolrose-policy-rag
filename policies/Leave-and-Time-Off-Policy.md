# Kolrose Limited - Leave and Time-Off Policy

**Document ID:** KOL-HR-002
**Version:** 2.1
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2024
**Department:** Human Resources

## 1. Annual Leave (Vacation)

### 1.1 Entitlement
Full-time permanent employees are entitled to annual leave as follows:

| Years of Service | Annual Leave Days |
|------------------|-------------------|
| 0-2 years | 15 working days |
| 3-5 years | 20 working days |
| 6-10 years | 25 working days |
| 10+ years | 30 working days |

### 1.2 Accrual
Annual leave accrues at the rate of 1.25 days per month for employees with 0-2 
years of service. Accrual rates adjust proportionally based on years of service.

### 1.3 Leave Scheduling
- Leave must be requested at least 14 calendar days in advance
- Maximum of 10 consecutive working days without Director approval
- December holiday leave is limited to 5 working days to ensure adequate staffing
- Leave during project critical phases may be denied at management discretion

### 1.4 Leave Carryover
A maximum of 5 unused leave days may be carried over to the following calendar year. 
Carried-over days must be used by March 31st of the following year or they are forfeited. 
No cash payment is made for unused leave days except upon termination.

### 1.5 Leave During Notice Period
Employees serving notice may be required to exhaust remaining leave during the 
notice period at the company's discretion.

## 2. Sick Leave

### 2.1 Entitlement
Employees are entitled to 12 working days of paid sick leave per calendar year. 
Sick leave accrues at 1 day per month of service.

### 2.2 Documentation Requirements
- Absence of 1-2 consecutive days: Self-certification via HR portal
- Absence of 3+ consecutive days: Medical certificate from registered practitioner required
- Absence exceeding 5 days: Company may request examination by company-nominated physician

### 2.3 Sick Leave During Probation
Probationary employees are entitled to sick leave but it is unpaid unless the 
employee has completed at least 3 months of service.

## 3. Maternity Leave

### 3.1 Entitlement
Female employees are entitled to 16 weeks (112 calendar days) of maternity leave 
with full pay, in accordance with Nigerian Labour Act provisions.

### 3.2 Conditions
- Employee must have completed 12 months of continuous service
- Leave may commence 2 weeks before expected delivery date
- Employee must provide medical certificate confirming pregnancy and expected delivery date
- Extension of up to 4 weeks unpaid leave may be granted on medical grounds

### 3.3 Return to Work
Returning employees are entitled to:
- Return to the same position or equivalent role
- Flexible working arrangements for first 3 months upon return
- Nursing breaks of 30 minutes twice daily for first 6 months

## 4. Paternity Leave

### 4.1 Entitlement
Male employees are entitled to 10 working days of paid paternity leave, to be taken 
within the first month of the child's birth or adoption.

### 4.2 Conditions
- Employee must have completed 6 months of continuous service
- Leave must be taken in a single block
- Employee must provide birth certificate or adoption documentation

## 5. Public Holidays

### 5.1 Nigerian Public Holidays
Kolrose Limited observes all Federal Government of Nigeria gazetted public holidays. 
Employees required to work on public holidays receive:
- Public holiday pay at 200% of normal rate, OR
- Alternative day off in lieu plus normal pay

### 5.2 Religious Holidays
Employees may request up to 2 additional days per year for religious observances 
beyond gazetted holidays. These days are unpaid unless taken from annual leave 
entitlement.

## 6. Leave Approval Process

### 6.1 Request Procedure
1. Employee submits leave request via HR Portal (hr.kolroselimited.com.ng) at least 14 days in advance
2. Line Manager reviews and approves/rejects within 3 working days
3. For leaves exceeding 10 days, Director approval is required
4. HR records all approved leave and updates accrual balances

### 6.2 Emergency Leave
In cases of emergency (death in family, medical emergency), employees should:
1. Notify line manager immediately by phone
2. Submit formal request within 24 hours
3. Provide supporting documentation within 7 days
