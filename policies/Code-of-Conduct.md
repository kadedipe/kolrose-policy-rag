# Kolrose Limited - Code of Conduct and Ethics

**Document ID:** KOL-HR-003
**Version:** 4.0
**Effective Date:** January 15, 2024
**Department:** Human Resources / Legal

## 1. Professional Conduct

### 1.1 General Standards
All employees of Kolrose Limited are expected to:
- Conduct themselves with honesty, integrity, and professionalism
- Comply with all applicable Nigerian laws and regulations
- Respect the rights, dignity, and diversity of colleagues, clients, and stakeholders
- Protect company assets, information, and reputation
- Avoid conflicts of interest between personal activities and company duties

### 1.2 Dress Code
Employees are expected to dress in business casual attire unless:
- Client meetings require formal business attire
- Specific project sites have alternative requirements
- Fridays are designated as traditional attire day (optional)

## 2. Confidentiality and Data Protection

### 2.1 Confidential Information
Employees must maintain strict confidentiality regarding:
- Client project details and deliverables
- Company financial information
- Trade secrets and proprietary methodologies
- Employee personal information
- Security protocols and access credentials

### 2.2 Nigeria Data Protection Regulation (NDPR) Compliance
Kolrose Limited is committed to compliance with the Nigeria Data Protection Regulation 
2019. Employees handling personal data must:
- Process data only for specified, legitimate purposes
- Ensure data accuracy and security
- Report data breaches immediately to the Data Protection Officer (DPO)
- Complete annual data protection training

### 2.3 Password and Credential Security
- Passwords must be at least 12 characters with mixed case, numbers, and symbols
- Passwords must be changed every 90 days
- Passwords must not be shared with any person, including IT staff
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is mandatory for all systems
- Any suspected credential compromise must be reported to IT Security immediately

## 3. Conflicts of Interest

### 3.1 Outside Employment
Employees must not engage in outside employment or business activities that:
- Compete with Kolrose Limited's business
- Use company resources, time, or information
- Conflict with the employee's duties and responsibilities
- Damage the company's reputation

### 3.2 Gifts and Hospitality
- Employees may accept nominal gifts valued below ₦10,000
- Gifts exceeding ₦10,000 must be declared to the Compliance Officer
- Cash gifts are strictly prohibited under any circumstances
- Hospitality from vendors/clients requires line manager approval

## 4. Anti-Corruption and Bribery

### 4.1 Zero Tolerance Policy
Kolrose Limited maintains a zero-tolerance approach to corruption, bribery, and 
facilitation payments, in compliance with:
- Nigerian Corrupt Practices and Other Related Offences Act 2000
- Economic and Financial Crimes Commission (EFCC) regulations
- UK Bribery Act 2010 (for international operations)

### 4.2 Reporting Obligations
Employees must immediately report any suspected corrupt practices to:
1. Line Manager (unless implicated)
2. Compliance Officer: compliance@kolroselimited.com.ng
3. Whistleblower Hotline: 0800-KOLROSE (anonymity guaranteed)

## 5. Disciplinary Framework

### 5.1 Offense Categories

| Category | Examples | First Offense |
|----------|----------|---------------|
| Minor | Lateness, dress code violation | Verbal warning |
| Moderate | Unauthorized absence, rudeness to client | Written warning |
| Serious | Fraud, harassment, confidentiality breach | Suspension/Termination |

### 5.2 Disciplinary Process
1. Investigation of alleged misconduct
2. Formal notification to employee (within 5 working days)
3. Disciplinary hearing with right to representation
4. Written decision within 3 working days of hearing
5. Right of appeal to Director within 7 working days
